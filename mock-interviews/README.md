# Mock Interviews

Mock interviews are the heart of the **Communication & Networking** category. You practice
technical interviewing as your honest, vulnerable self with another person — a classmate,
a TA, or (ideally) a real professional in industry.

## What to do

- **Connect with Class** — join the class Teams and introduce yourself.
- **Weekly mock interviews** — do a mock interview most weeks of the semester. Early ones
  can be with classmates; later ones should increasingly be **reports** on interviews you
  conducted, ideally with people in industry.
- **Real Professional Mock Interview** — complete at least one mock interview with a
  working professional (see [`linkedin-outreach.md`](linkedin-outreach.md) for how to reach
  out) and submit a report.

## Recording completion

Each mock interview is a **Canvas assignment**. Submit a short report: who you interviewed
with, when, what problem(s) you worked on, what feedback you received, and a **self-rating**:

- **1** — Showed up, went poorly.
- **2** — Got to a solution.
- **3** — Collaborated well and definitely want to hire!

> Previously these were true/false self-report quizzes in Learning Suite. For Fall 2026 they
> are Canvas assignments so the report content is captured directly.

## Extra credit

Real interview reports and real offer reports are available as extra credit — submit them
as their own Canvas assignments when you have them.
