# Mock Interviews

You practice technical interviewing as your honest, vulnerable self with another person — a classmate,
a TA, or (ideally) a real professional in industry.

## What to do

- **Reach out to someone!**
- Set a time and place when you can do the interview. Maybe right after class? Ideally in person at a whiteboard. 
- **Interviewer**: Give the person a problem that they could potentially finish or get close in 15 minutes. Read or describe the problem to them and then watch them solve it. Answer questions as they go (ideally you have already done this problem). Give them encouragement and keep an eye on the time. You minimally need to wait until the problem is solved **or** 15 minutes. Don't let this go longer than 30 minutes and 15 minutes is probably best to cut it short (they don't have to finish!).
- **Interviewee**: Be curious. Be happy. Have fun with the problem. Thank the interviewer!

This assignment is fulfilled by being the **interviewee**, however, being an **interviewer** counts towards your 4 hours of collaborative time. You'll definitely learn a lot about interviewing by being the interviewer.


## Real Professional Mock Interview
Complete at least one mock interview with a working professional (see [`linkedin-outreach.md`](linkedin-outreach.md) for how to reach out) and submit a report.

## Recording completion

Each mock interview is a **Canvas assignment**. Submit a short report: who you interviewed
with, when, what problem(s) you worked on, what feedback you received, and a **self-rating**:

- **1** — Showed up, went poorly.
- **2** — Got to a solution.
- **3** — Collaborated well and went great!

## Extra credit

Real interview reports and real offer reports are available as extra credit — submit them
as their own Canvas assignments when you have them.
