# Contacting Professionals on LinkedIn for Mock Interviews

Use these templates to ask a professional for a short informational chat + mock technical
interview. Make them your own.

## Example 1 — a BYU alum at a company you're interested in (e.g., Lucid)

> Hello **\<name\>**! I am **\<your name\>**, a current computer science student at BYU. I
> was wondering if you could spare 25–35 minutes to tell me a little about what it's like to
> work at **Lucid**, and also do a mock technical interview with me? It's a requirement for
> my CS 393 class, but I'd also like to get to know more about Lucid and prepare for
> interviews. **\<suggest a couple of times, or share a Calendly/Microsoft Bookings link\>**

## Example 2 — anyone at any company (e.g., Microsoft)

> Hello **\<name\>**! I am **\<your name\>**, a current computer science student at BYU. I
> was wondering if you could spare 25–35 minutes to tell me a little about what it's like to
> work at **\<company\>**, and also do a mock technical interview with me? It's a requirement
> for my CS 393 class, but I'd also like to get to know more about **\<company\>** and prepare
> for interviews. **\<suggest a couple of times, or share a scheduling link\>**

## Notes

- **Make it your own** — customize it to your voice. 🙂
- BYU alumni (people with BYU on their LinkedIn) may be more receptive, but people are
  people — you might find some who are super helpful. It reportedly takes about **10 cold
  messages to find 1 person who responds**, so reach out at least **a month ahead** of when
  you need to meet.
- **Represent BYU well.** After interacting with you, ideally they 1) want to hire you (or
  recommend you to their company) and 2) are enthusiastic about meeting more students.
- **Ask them to give you a technical question** to work on. Zoom or Microsoft Teams works
  well. Afterward, ask for feedback on what you could do to get a job at their company —
  they may not do the hiring, but they got hired themselves and can help you.
